Languages Spoken in the U.S.

By Eugenia Blaubach

According to the U.S. Census Bureau, 13 percent of the U.S. population is foreign born. These individuals assimilate to American life but naturally retain the culture, traditions and language from their native countries. The U.S. does not have an official language, yet the growth of languages other than English has sparked pro-English movements. While speaking English does facilitate assimilation and may lead to more economic success, the pro-English movement has generated backlash defending immigrants' right to speak in their native tongues.

However, a significant amount of data reveals that as immigrant families become more established in the country, they stop using their native language. The majority of foreign-born individuals living in the U.S. are from Latin America [51.8 percent], but only 13 percent of Americans speak Spanish in their homes. Additionally, if an individual speaks a foreign language in his or her homes, it does not imply a lack of fluency in English.
