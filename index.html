Languages Spoken in the U.S.

By Eugenia Blaubach

According to the U.S. Census Bureau, 13 percent of the U.S. population is foreign born. While these individuals assimilate to American life, it is natural for them to retain the culture and traditions from their native countries. However, a closer look at the data reveals that as immigrant families become more established in the country, they stop using their native language. Does not imply a lack of fluency in English
